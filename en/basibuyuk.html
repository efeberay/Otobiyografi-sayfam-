History of Basibuyuk

Basibuyuk Neighborhood is considered one of Maltepe's oldest settlements. Sources mention a settlement predating the Ottomans and note that during the 14th-century Battle of Pelekanon (Maltepe) the area was referred to as "Basbug village." Over time that name evolved into today's Basibuyuk.

The neighborhood formed around Basibuyuk Central Mosque—once a Byzantine church converted into a mosque—and its location on the slopes of Kayisdagi has carried both strategic and residential importance throughout history. In the 20th century Basibuyuk stayed a village for a long time, with agriculture and livestock as the main livelihood.

In the Republican era the population slowly grew; first Kayisdagi water was brought in, then electricity and television in the 1960s, later telephone service, and natural gas in the 2000s, gradually transforming Basibuyuk into a modern neighborhood. The opening of Sureyyapasa Hospital was a key milestone that increased both recognition of the area and the pace of development.

Geographically, Basibuyuk sits on the slopes of Kayisdagi at a higher elevation than sea level, so it has cooler and milder weather compared with Istanbul's center. Nearby water basins, forested areas, and rich vegetation help the neighborhood keep a "next to the forest, not in concrete" character today.

Basibuyuk Timeline

  1. Around 1329

    Referred to as "Basbug village" during the Battle of Pelekanon.

  2. Early 20th century

    A small village-scale settlement focused on agriculture and livestock.

  3. 1950s

    Construction accelerates with the opening of Sureyyapasa Hospital.

  4. 1960–2000

    Water, electricity, telephone, and natural gas investments strengthen the neighborhood's infrastructure.

  5. Today

    A neighborhood where forest, hospital, university campuses, and modern housing meet.
